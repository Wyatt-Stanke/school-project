The Old Way (Gianna)

A long time ago, nobody had technology to use. People thought new inventions were hammers made out of a rock and a stick. No one knew about technology (no, they didn't know about TikTok or Instagram. Not even Wi-Fi. Crazy, right?)

Everyone back then thought they were geniuses if they used a stick, and a rock to make a slingshot. Which was probably really interesting for people back then. But our interesting nowadays is the iPhone 13. Now try to compare an iPhone 13 to a slingshot made of rocks and sticks.

The only ways people really survived back then was because of huts. No one really had houses made of brick or cement. They had huts made of sticks, straw, leaves, etc. Now this wasn't an easy task to be living outdoors 24/7, but they were used to it. Imagine living out in the wild with barely anything but animals which were food, and some (hopefully) clean lakes which was a water resource?

The New Way (Wyatt)

Nowadays, everything is automated. Some tools are made with a CNC machine, where you take a slab of material and cut it down until it represents what you want. A computer gets an object that it needs to cut a block down to and it computes paths for the drill to take to make what you want. This is pretty wasteful since it can be hard to reuse the scraps that are sawed off.

Other tools are made using a 3D printer, which starts with a roll of material, and constructively makes the thing by making layers of materials. This is *extremely* slow, but due to new innovations in materials, a resin printer can go almost 20x faster because instead of doing one line of one layer at a time, it goes 1 layer at a time.

However, for something that is probably used more today, people make tools assisted with machines that can help them. The machines can make the tools stronger and more durable.
